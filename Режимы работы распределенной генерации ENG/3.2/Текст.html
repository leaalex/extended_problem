Example. It is necessary to determine the major geometric dimensions of the main elements of the high pressure fuel pump for the diesel engine, if the following parameters are known:

Cylinder power of the engine \(N_c = 16.67 \ \mathrm{kW}\);

Crankshaft speed \(n = 2200 \ \mathrm{r/min}\);

Engine strokeness \(\tau = 4\);

Fuel density \(\rho_f = 850 \ \mathrm{kg/m^3}\);

Specific fuel consumption \(g_e = 245 \ \mathrm{g/(kW \cdot h)}\);

Fuel supply coefficient \(k = 1.35\);

Injection duration \(\varphi = 25 \) degrees;

Supply coefficient \(\eta = 0.9\);

Plunger speed \(s_p = 2 \ \mathrm{m/s}\);

Solution

Volumetric cycle fuel supply:

\[ V_c = \cfrac{g_e N_c \tau}{120 n \rho_m} \cdot 10^6 = \cfrac{245 \cdot 16.67 \cdot 4}{120 \cdot 2200 \cdot 850} \cdot 10^6 = 72.8 \ \mathrm{mm^3/cycle}.\]

The diameter of the fuel pump plunger:

\[ d_p = \sqrt{\cfrac{4}{\pi} \cdot k \cdot \cfrac{V_c 6n}{\varphi \cdot \eta \cdot c_p} \cdot 10^{-3} } = \sqrt{\cfrac{4}{3.14} \cdot 1.35 \cdot \cfrac{72.8 \cdot 6 \cdot 2200}{25 \cdot 0.9 \cdot 2} \cdot 10^{-3}} = 6.06 \approx 6.0 \ \mathrm{mm}.\]

Active plunger move:

\[ h = \cfrac{V_c}{ \frac{\pi \cdot d^2_p}{4 \cdot \eta} } = \cfrac{72.8}{ \frac{3.14 \cdot 6^2}{4 \cdot 0.9} } = 2.86 \ \mathrm{mm}.\]

The diameter of the intake and blocked off bores:

\[d_c = (0.25 ... 0.35) \cdot d_p = 0.3 \cdot d_p = 0.3 \cdot 6 = 1.8 \ \mathrm{mm} .\]

The minimum necessary full plunger move:

\[H_{min} = h + 2d_c = 2.86 + 2 \cdot 1.8 = 6.46 \ \mathrm{mm}\]

Full plunger move:

set \( H = 6.5 \ \mathrm{mm}\).
