Example. Determine the amount of lubricant pumped through the engine and the volume of lubricant poured into the system for the diesel engine with the following parameters:

Engine power \(N_e = 66.2 \ \mathrm{kW}\).

Lubricant temperature at the engine intake \(T_1 = 75 \ ^{\circ}C\).

Lubricant temperature at the engine exhaust \(T_2 = 90 \ ^{\circ}C\).

Yield factor \(\xi = 2.5\).

Specific heat sink in lubricant: \(0.885 \ \mathrm{kJ/(kg \cdot h)}\).

Lubricant heat capacity for \(T_{hc} = 1.9 \ \mathrm{kJ/(kg \cdot K)}\).

Type of lubricant system: a wet sump system.

Solution

Lubricant temperature difference at the engine exhaust and intake

\(\Delta T = T2 – T1 = 90 – 75 = 15 ^{\circ}C\).

The amount of lubricant pumped through the engine:

\( G_L = \cfrac{\xi \cdot N_e \cdot q}{\Delta \cdot c_l} = \cfrac{2.5 \cdot 66.2 \cdot 0.885}{15 \cdot 1.9} = 5.14 \ \mathrm{kg/s}\).

The volume of lubricant poured into the system:

\(V_s = (0.07 ... 0.21) \cdot N_e = 0.15 \cdot 66.2 = 9.93 \ \mathrm{l}\).
