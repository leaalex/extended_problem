Example 1. Determine the specific effective and isentropic operation of the turbine if the gas temperature in front of the turbine \(T^*_G = 1600 \ \mathrm{K}\), the gas temperature behind the turbine \(T^*_T = 1200 \ \mathrm{K}\), turbine efficiency \(\eta^*_T = 0.90\), the isobaric heat capacity of gases \(C_{HC} = 1.157 \ \mathrm{kJ/(kg \cdot K)}\).

Solution

Isentropic work is calculated by the formula:

\[ L^*_{TS} = C_{HC} (T^*_G - T^*_T) = 1.157(1600 - 1200) = 462.8 \ \mathrm{kJ/kg}.\]

Specific effective operation:

\[ L^*_T = L^*_{TS} \cdot \eta^*_T = 462.8 \cdot 0.9 = 416.52 \ \mathrm{kJ/kg}.\]

Example 2. Determine the power developed by the GTE turbine, if the gas flow through the turbine is \(G_G = 100 \ kg/s\), the expansion in the turbine \(\pi^*_T = 3\), the gas temperature in front of the turbine \(T^*_G = 1500 \ \mathrm{K}\), the isentropic efficiency of the turbine \(\eta^*_T = 0.90\) (set \(C_{HC} = 1.157 \ \mathrm{kJ/(kg \cdot K)}\), \(k_G = 1.33\) ).

Solution

Specific effective operation of the turbine:

\[ L_{T} = C_{HC} \cdot T^*_G (1 - 1/ \pi_{T}^{\frac{k_G-1}{k_G}}) \cdot \eta^*_T = 1.157 \cdot 1500 (1 - 1/3^{\frac{1.3 - 1}{1.3}}) \cdot 0.9 = 349.74 \ \mathrm{kJ/kg}.\]

GTE turbine power:

\[ N_T = G_G \cdot L_T = 349.74 \cdot 100 = 34974 \ \mathrm{kW}.\]
