Example 1. A four-stroke, four-cylinder gasoline engine develops an effective power of \(53 \ \mathrm{kW}\) at a crankshaft speed of \(5400 \ min^{-1}\). Mean indicator pressure \(p_i = 1.06 \ \mathrm{MPa}\), mean mechanical loss pressure \(p_m = 0.31 \ \mathrm{MPa}\). The piston stroke is \(80 \ \mathrm{mm}\). Determine the engine cylinder diameter \(D\).

Solution

It is possible to determine the cylinder diameter value from the equation for calculating the effective engine power:

\[ N_e = \cfrac{p_e V_h n i}{30 \tau} = \cfrac{p_e n i}{30 \tau} \cdot \cfrac{\pi D^2 \cdot S}{4}.\]

Therefore the cylinder diameter is:

\[D = \sqrt{\cfrac{120 \cdot N_e \cdot \tau }{p_e \cdot \pi \cdot S \cdot n \cdot i}}.\]

In this formula, all quantities are known, except for the mean effective pressure \(p_e\). The mean effective pressure of the cycle is:

\(p_e = p_i - p_m = 1.06 - 0.31 = 0.75 \ \mathrm{MPa}.\)

When determining the cylinder diameter, the piston stroke must be substituted in decimeters (dm), since the cylinder capacity must be substituted in liters \((dm^3)\) in this formula

\( D = \sqrt{\cfrac{120 \cdot N_e \cdot \tau }{p_e \cdot \pi \cdot S \cdot n \cdot i}} = D = \sqrt{\cfrac{120 \cdot 53 \cdot 4 }{0.75 \cdot 3.14 \cdot 0.8 \cdot 5400 \cdot 4}} = 0.79 \ \mathrm{dm}\) (or \(79\) \(\mathrm{mm}\)).

Example 2. A four-stroke, eight-cylinder \(200 \ \mathrm{kW}\) gasoline engine at a crankshaft speed of \(4200 \ min^{-1}\) has a specific effective fuel consumption \(g_e = 300 \ \mathrm{g/(kW \cdot h)}\). The cylinder diameter is \(108 \ \mathrm{mm}\), the stroke is \(95 \ \mathrm{mm}\). The mean mechanical loss pressure \(p_m = 0.187 \ \mathrm{MPa}\). Determine the indicator values of the operating cycle: mean indicator pressure \(pi\), indicator efficiency \(\eta_i\), specific indicator fuel consumption \(g_i\).

Solution

The solution can be started by determining the mean effective pressure of the working cycle: \( p_e = \cfrac{N_e 30 \tau}{V_h ni} \). The cylinder capacity is unknown.

The cylinder capacity is:

\( V_h = \cfrac{\pi D^2}{4} \cdot S = \cfrac{3.14 \cdot 1.08^2}{4} \cdot 0.95 = 0.87 \ \mathrm{l \ (dm^3)}\).

Then the mean effective pressure of the cycle is:

\( p_e = \cfrac{N_e 30 \tau}{V_h n i } = \cfrac{200 \cdot 30 \cdot 4}{0.87 \cdot 4200 \cdot 8} = 0.82 \ \mathrm{MPa}\).

The mean indicator pressure of the cycle is:

\( p_i = p_e + p_m = 0.82 + 0.187 = 1.007 \ \mathrm{MPa}\).

Net efficiency, characterizing the proportion of the fuel combustion heat, which is converted into effective operation in the engine is:

\( \eta_e = \cfrac{3600}{Hu \cdot g_e} = \cfrac{3600}{44 \cdot 300} = 0.273. \)

The cycle mechanical efficiency is:

\(\eta_{Ð¼} = p_e/p_i = 0.82/1.007 = 0.814.\)

The cycle indicator efficiency is:

\(\eta_{i} = \eta_e/\eta_m = 0.273/0.814 = 0.335.\)

The specific indicator fuel consumption

\( g_i = \cfrac{3600}{Hu \cdot \eta_i} = \cfrac{3600}{44 \cdot 0.335} = 244 \ \mathrm{g/(kW \cdot h)}.\)
