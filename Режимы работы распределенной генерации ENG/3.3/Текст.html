Example 1. Determine the fuel consumption per hour (kerosene) through a gas turbine engine, if the air consumption through the combustor \(G_A = 100 \ \mathrm{kg/s}\), heat, supplied to \(1 \ \mathrm{kg}\) of air \(q_1 = 700 \ \mathrm{kJ/kg}\), combustion efficiency \( \eta_c = 0.99\), fuel heat capacity \(H_u = 43000 \ \mathrm{kJ/kg}\) are known.

Solution

The fuel consumption per hour:

\( G_T = \cfrac{G_B \cdot q_1}{H_u \cdot \eta_c} = \cfrac{100 \cdot 700}{43000 \cdot 0.99} = 1.64 \ \mathrm{kg/s} \).
